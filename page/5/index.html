Shallow vision and storytelling makes the latest “Macbeth” a weak cinematic exploration

Mon, Sep 21, 2015 Justin Kurzel’s “Macbeth” opens with the funeral of Macbeth and Lady Macbeth’s child. It’s common for writers to add scenes to Shakespeare that help render their telling unique and develop strands of his work that have yet to be explored. The decision to include this is a bold move and suggests a particular subtext to the interpretation that will follow. That it never is, through dialogue or performance, is emblematic of an interpretation lacking in direction and depth.
